Nan Tian

I'm a enthusiastic robotics innovator and embodied AI researcher in San Francisco. My goal is to make emerging robotics AI technologies accessible to a broader audience through Cloud Robotics, robotics learning, and physical human-robot interactions (pHRI). I aim to enable our society to utilize, understand, and interact with robots in an efficient, effective, and safe manner.

For a decade, I have been a lead research scientist and a principle investigator in various industrial research institutes at Bosch, Siemens, Nikon, and CloudMinds. I closely collaborate with robotics AI entities at universities including BAIR at UC Berkeley, RI at CMU, and CAM at CMU. I have also worked with many industrial partners via ARM Institute.

I did my PhD at UC Berkeley in Computer Science, where I was advised by Prof. Somayah Sojoudi collaborating with Prof. Ken Goldberg on Cloud-Edge Hybrid Robotics for Physical Human Robot Interaction

Before robotics, I was a seasoned researcher in brain machine interface (BMI) and biomedical imaging. I worked with Prof. Philip Sabes and Dr. Krystof Bankiewicz at UCSF Neuroscience on interventional MRI for optogenetics in monkeys. I also studied at CWRU under Prof. Dominique Durand on electrical stimulations to the brain and with Dr. Kingman Strohl on chronic epilespy and sleep monitorings.

Dynamic Visual Servo for Robust Prism Insertion
Nan Tian
Nikon CTO Presentation & Demo 2020
Nikon Research Corporation of America
Full Video

Position based visual servoing to perform prism insertion. Controlling the robot arm based on relative 3D pose between objects (instead of relative to the arm end-effector), the insertions are robust and precise even when either object slips or moves at any time.

Nan Tian
