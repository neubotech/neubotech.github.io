Nan Tian

I'm an enthusiastic roboticist and embodied AI researcher based in San Francisco. I develop scalable dynamic visual manipulation techniques to enable intuitive physical human-robot interaction (pHRI). I focus on agility, interpretability, reliability, and data efficient imitation learning. Some of my works are inspired by neuroscience and psychology.

I have been a lead research scientist and a principle investigator for various industrial research institutes since 2016, working for Bosch, Siemens, Nikon, and CloudMinds. I closely collaborate with robotics AI researchers at universities, from BAIR at UC Berkeley, CAM at USC, and RI at CMU. I have also worked with many industrial partners in the past via ARM Institute.

I did my PhD at UC Berkeley in Computer Science, where I was advised by Prof. Somayah Sojoudi in collaboration with Prof. Ken Goldberg on Cloud-Edge Hybrid Robotics for Physical Human Robot Interaction.

Before robotics, I was a seasoned researcher in brain machine interface (BMI) and biomedical imaging at UCSF. I worked with Prof. Philip Sabes and Dr. Krystof Bankiewicz in Neuroscience on MRI-guided injection of optogenetics to monkeys. I also worked with Dr. James Barkovich and Prof. Duan Xu in Radiology on clinical MRI scans of newborn brain.

Research Interests

Agile robotics control for dynamic pHRI, Interactive 2D/3D robotics vision, VR/AR Teleoperation, Cloud Robotics (AI with reliability), data efficient imitation learning, and more recently, interpretable end2end robotics learning (coming soon).

I find highlighted projects fun and insightful. Therefore, I spent a lot of time on them. I hope you like them too.
